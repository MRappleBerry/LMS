Can you explain the doctrine of stare decisis?
AI
Absolutely! Stare decisis, Latin for "to stand by things decided," is the doctrine of precedent. It means courts should follow established precedents when deciding cases. There are two key types:

Vertical stare decisis: Lower courts must follow decisions of higher courts in the same jurisdiction.

Horizontal stare decisis: Courts typically follow their own prior decisions.

This doctrine promotes consistency, predictability, and fairness in the legal system.
You
How does it apply to Miranda v. Arizona?
AI
Great connection! Miranda v. Arizona (1966) is itself a landmark precedent that established Miranda Rights. Under stare decisis, all lower courts and the states must follow this Supreme Court decision. This means:

• Police must read suspects their rights before custodial interrogation • Failure to do so renders statements inadmissible • This applies nationwide as binding precedent

It's a perfect example of vertical stare decisis in action.

Marbury v. Madison

📅 1803 🏢 Supreme Court of the United States ⚖️ Constitutional Law

This case involves William Marbury, who was appointed as a justice of the peace but did not receive his commission, and Secretary of State James Madison, who refused to deliver it. Marbury brought suit directly to the Supreme Court requesting a writ of mandamus to compel Madison to deliver the commission.

Chief Justice John Marshall wrote the decision holding that while Marbury was entitled to his commission and the law providing a remedy (mandamus) was appropriate, the Supreme Court lacked the authority to issue such a writ under the original jurisdiction granted to it by Article III of the Constitution.

Marshall further held that the statute that purported to grant the Supreme Court this jurisdiction conflicted with the Constitution. In the pivotal portion of his decision, Marshall asserted that when an act of Congress conflicts with the Constitution, the Court has a duty to determine which is supreme and that the Constitution is the supreme law of the land.

This decision established the doctrine of judicial review, giving the Court the power to declare acts of Congress unconstitutional. Though the immediate effect of the decision was to prevent Marbury from getting his commission, the long-term consequence was to establish the judiciary as an independent and co-equal branch of government.
